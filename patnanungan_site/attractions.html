Beaches & Attractions

From pristine white-sand shores to secluded coves — Patnanungan's coastline is its greatest treasure.

📍 Barangay Poblacion, Patnanungan Island, Quezon Province

Villa Karrine Beach Resort

Patnanungan's most celebrated stretch of shoreline — fine white sand meeting calm azure waters that shift from pale turquoise to deep blue at the reef edge. The island's primary beach destination and natural gathering place for visitors and locals alike.

Ideal for swimming, leisurely wading, and unwinding under coconut shade. At low tide the exposed sandbar extends further, offering a walking path flanked by warm shallow waters. Sunrise here is particularly stunning.

📍 Patnanungan Island, Eastern Quezon province, Philippines

Patnanungan Island

Tucked away in the eastern reaches of Quezon province, Patnanungan Island is a hidden, shoe-shaped paradise waiting for travelers who crave raw, untouched destinations. This off-the-grid sanctuary is defined by remarkably shallow, knee-deep coastal waters that roll gently onto unpolished white sandbars like Labni Beach. Adventure seekers can snorkel through its vast, underwater coral gardens or feast on an abundance of ultra-fresh lobsters and crabs caught straight from the Pacific Ocean. Serving as a quiet, slower-paced alternative to the tourist tracks, it stands as the ultimate launchpad for exploring the untamed beauty of the Polillo Group of Islands.

📍 Barangay Malapad, Real, Quezon Province, Philippines

Balagbag Falls

Tucked away just off the scenic coastal highway, Balagbag Falls is a majestic two-tiered natural wonder surrounded by the lush rainforests of the Sierra Madre. Adventure seekers can get their adrenaline pumping by cliff-jumping into the deep, refreshing plunge pool of the five-meter-high lower tier. If you prefer a tranquil escape, you can climb the carved steps to the towering 25-meter upper tier, where clear mountain water cascades gracefully over massive rock formations. This breathtaking oasis perfectly bridges the gap between mountain and sea, making it the ultimate refreshing detour before you catch your ferry out to the islands.

📍 Surrounding Waters · Various Islands

Surrounding Islands & Coastal Areas

Patnanungan Island is a hidden paradise waiting to be explored, defined by its pristine, shallow coastal ecosystems and a rustic marine environment located within the Polillo archipelago. Surrounded by the rich fishing grounds of the Philippine Sea and Lamon Bay, the island’s clear waters are absolutely packed with diverse marine life and expansive coral gardens that sit right below the surface. Its unique shoreline features remarkably shallow, knee-deep waters that stretch far out from the coast, leading into unpolished white-sand beaches like Labni that remain beautifully undeveloped. Positioned near neighboring gems like Jomalig and Burdeos, Patnanungan serves as the perfect launchpad for off-the-grid, raw island-hopping adventures far away from crowded tourist circuits.
