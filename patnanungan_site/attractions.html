Beaches & Attractions

From pristine white-sand shores to secluded coves — Patnanungan's coastline is its greatest treasure.

🌊

📍 Patnanungan · Main Beach

White Beach

Patnanungan's most celebrated stretch of shoreline — fine white sand meeting calm azure waters that shift from pale turquoise to deep blue at the reef edge. The island's primary beach destination and natural gathering place for visitors and locals alike.

Ideal for swimming, leisurely wading, and unwinding under coconut shade. At low tide the exposed sandbar extends further, offering a walking path flanked by warm shallow waters. Sunrise here is particularly stunning.

🏊 Swimming☀️ Sunbathing🌅 Sunrise Viewing👣 Sandbar Walking📷 Photography
🌅

📍 Patnanungan · West Side

Compra Beach

Named after the local trading activity historically tied to this area, Compra Beach is beloved for its spectacular sunset views. Facing westward, the beach becomes a canvas of warm oranges and purples as the sun dips toward the horizon each evening.

Slightly coarser sand and more dramatic rock formations than White Beach lend it a wilder, more photogenic character. Fishermen's boats moored along the shore add an authentic cultural dimension.

🌇 Sunset Viewing📷 Photography⛵ Boat Watching🚶 Beach Walk
🐚

📍 Patnanungan · Southeast

Pulang Lupa Beach

Pulang Lupa — literally "red earth" — earns its name from distinctive reddish-tinged sand and soil that color its shoreline. This geological quirk makes it unlike any other beach in the Polillo group, attracting curious travelers and nature enthusiasts.

Beyond its unique visual character, Pulang Lupa sits adjacent to some of the island's healthiest coral patches, often rewarding snorkelers with colorful reef fish, sea urchins, and occasional sea turtles.

📍 Surrounding Waters · Various Islands

Surrounding Islands & Coastal Areas

Patnanungan's waters are dotted with smaller islets, sandbars, and rock formations accessible by bangka (outrigger boat). Island hopping tours organized by local boatmen take visitors on half-day or full-day excursions to satellite destinations.

The mangrove-lined channels between islands are particularly interesting for nature lovers — home to migratory birds, mudskippers, and juvenile fish that use the mangrove roots as nurseries.
