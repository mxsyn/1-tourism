Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the ferry take from Real to Patnanungan?
The ferry crossing from Real, Quezon to Patnanungan takes approximately 3–4 hours depending on sea conditions and the type of vessel. Ferries typically depart early in the morning. Always confirm current schedules with the Real municipal port.
Is there an ATM or internet on the island?
Currently, there are no ATMs on Patnanungan. Bring sufficient Philippine Peso cash for your entire stay. Mobile data signal is available in some areas but can be limited — download offline maps and important information before departing for the island.
What's the best season to visit?
November to May is the recommended period. December to February offers the calmest seas and clearest water. June to October is typhoon and monsoon season — sea travel can be disrupted, so plan around the amihan season for the safest experience.
Do I need to book accommodations in advance?
Advance booking is strongly recommended, especially during peak season (December–February) and around festival dates. Most accommodations don't have online booking — contact the municipal tourism office for a referral to available lodgings.
Can I go island hopping on my own?
You can arrange bangka rentals with local boatmen at the port. It's highly recommended to hire a guide who knows the waters well, especially if you're visiting outer islets or snorkeling sites. Always inform someone at your accommodation before heading out.
